Chapter 5

*@* I walked through

the door you slipped

out of the light.

No reason why,

no thread to tie

the silent night.


I brought you a gift:

a golden box

with inner blue—

but never reached

to hold your hands.


You gave me a taste,

then left me crazy.

I killed the flame

with my own hands,

froze the beating

inside my chest.


No one to blame,

no fight to name—

only shadows I caress.


Words lost in the wind,

meant forever,

but nothing's left.


I kept the lie,

wore the mask.

I held the weight—

I didn’t ask.


You gave me high,

then took the sky.

And I let all

blissfully die.

Chapter 13

*@* Black touches and thick caps throws January,

pierces what can, glass welds.

Still will withstand snow phenomena,

marble soon will emigrate March.


At crow's cawing like rivers breath,

will fade light to shadows pale.

A little will dream, exile across sky,

before in air fly May.


Oh, how here grows in fresh tone June,

blows wind of herbs after girl.

Orchards sprinkle with flower, where groves my,

lips your cherries themselves will not ashame.


Before dust moon in windows sprinkles August,

linden tea gleaner sips.

Sickle in money yellow grain cuts,

on stove will rest hot September.


Increasingly rainy and gold of leaves falls October,

hear clods on furrow.

Candlestick trembling circles draws,

sand on pavement December.

Chapter 15

*@* In the human mind

between the multiplicity of sentences.


In the eyes of laughter

flashes blind me.

Of the magical world,

perhaps I possess signs,

causes without effect.


For a year of new ones,

I reflect on glass

the lives of my children.

Chapter 16

*@* An aquarium full of colors,

a river flowing,

the last pixel fading.

On a night like this,

you can't sleep,

a narcotic,

invisible stroll.


An unwanted black and white,

a block of icy return,

circled and disappeared

into the abyss.


An aquarium full of illusions,

the lack of light,

the denseness doesn't bother.

This woman,

whom everyone stares at,

isn't all that pretty,

her lips drunk,

swollen from curses.

She needs a breath

to see the unknown colors anew.


This woman opened her legs

and saw the river

of unwanted children born.

Now I will drown

in the quiet beating of my heart,

no noble angels.

I swallow oxygen,

it revives,

it lulls,

it kills.
